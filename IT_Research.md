## The Executive Summary 

The "Right to Information" is being viewed primarily as a legal obligation. This research was aimed at shifting that perspective, arguing that Access to Information is fundamentally a data management challenge. During my time with the Commission, I analyzed how digital systems serve as the bridge between bureaucratic databases and the public. We mbvmbv't just moving from paper to screens. We are moving from "hidden data" to "actionable insights." If the IT infrastructure is weak, the legal right is effectively unenforceable.

## Objectives 

1. To investigate the importance of ICT tools in facilitating ease of requesting for, and the supply of information by duty bearers.

2. To identify technological tools adopted by public institutions to process and provide information to applicants.

3. To provide recommendations that can enhance/improve access to and providing information via technological platforms.

## Methodology 

**Research Design**
- Descriptive research design utilized.
- Aimed at generating quantitative data.

**Sampling Method**
- Purposive and convenience sampling used.
- Ensured representation of relevant stakeholders.

**Data Collection**
- Electronic questionnaires employed. 
- Convenient method for gathering responses.

**Data Analysis**
- Utilised Power BI for analysis
- Presented data graphically 

**Sample Size**
- Total sample size: 600 (Information Officers: 295, Applicants:305)

## Conclusion
1. **Importance of ICT Tools**
- Majority of respondents recognize the significance of ICT tools in facilitating easy access to information.
- Confidence in ICT's ability to promote accessibility emphasizes its importance in improving vailability and accessibility of information.

2. **Advantages and Concerns of ICT**
- Advantages include increased accountability, accessibility, efficiency, and convenience.
- Concerns include unequal access, digital literacy gaps, dependency risks, and privacy/security concerns.

3. **Technological Tools in Information Exchange**
- Various tools such as chat boxes, web portals, email, and online apps are used.
- Email is the most common method, but a shift towards online databases is observed.

4. **Satisfaction and Preferences**
- Satisfaction levels with ICT-based information varied, with a sizable portion expressing high satisfaction.
- Preferences for information-giving and receiving differ, with traditional mail and web portals emerging as popular methods.

5. **Trust in Technological Platforms**
- Majority perceive ICT-based sources of information as more dependable than traditional sources.
- Access to ICT platforms improves capacity to obtain necessary information, highlighting technology's beneficial effects on accessibility.

## Recommendations

1. **Enhancing User Experience and Digital Literacy**
- Improve user interface of ICT platforms for better user-friendliness and intuitiveness.
- Prioritise initiatives to expand digital literacy to ensure effective utilization of ICT tools for information exchange.

2. **Integration of AI and Automation**
- Explore integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and automation technologies to streamline information processing tasks.
- AI-driven solutions can enhance efficiency and accuracy in handling information requests and responses.

3. **Strengthening Cybersecurity Measures**
- Implement robust encryption protocols and regular security audits to protect sensitive information.
- Conduct training programs to raise awareness about cybersecurity best practices among stakeholders. 

4. **Fostering Interoperability**
- Foster interoperability between different ICT platforms and tools to facilitate seamless information exchange.
- Collaboration among public institutions and private sector stakeholders can establish common standards and protocols for interoperability. 

5. **Monitoring and Evaluation**
- Establish regular monitoring and evaluation mechanisms to assess the effectiveness of ICT-based information exchange initiatives.
-  Collect feedback from users and stakeholders to identify areas for improvement and refine strategies to meet evolving public needs.
